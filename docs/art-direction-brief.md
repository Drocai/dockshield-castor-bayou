# Art Direction Brief

## Working Visual Promise

DockShield should look like storm-rescue superhero fiction colliding with contaminated southern waterway horror.

## First Slice Environment

Castor Bayou should communicate:

- wet wood docks
- toxic water
- storm sky
- utility floodlights
- fishing gear turned tactical
- old fish-processing infrastructure
- mud, reeds, warning signs, and industrial runoff

## Palette Target

Avoid making everything one color.

Use:

- storm gray
- emergency orange
- toxic green
- muddy brown as support, not the whole palette
- cold blue water highlights
- red warning lights

## Hero Silhouettes

### The Reel

Readable as rescue bruiser and gadget fisherman.

Signals:

- rod/reel rig
- rescue gear
- hook/grapple line
- public-facing hero posture

### The Fly

Readable as stealth recon and unsettling precision specialist.

Signals:

- thin line tools
- bug-eye or visor motif
- quiet silhouette
- vibration/marking visual language

### Lilly Loch

Readable as petite human form with overwhelming swamp-force potential.

Locked concept:

- human Lilly is petite, 22, high red pigtails, freckles, muddy country-girl charm
- monster Lilly is huge, terrifying, beautiful, swamp-Loch-Ness She-Hulk energy

## First Art Deliverables

- bayou dock prop kit
- toxic water material reference
- fishing reel gadget prop
- vine bind VFX concept
- fly mark VFX concept
- boss silhouette concept
- command HUD style reference
